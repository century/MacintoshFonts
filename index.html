Chicago

Chicago was a typeface designed by Susan Kare for Apple Inc. for the Macintosh. It was used as the system typeface between system 1.0 and 7.6. It's design is that of a high-contrast sans-serif typeface. It was chosen because it offered very good legibility as well as being able to be greyed out by removing every other pixel.

New York

New York was a Times New Roman-inspired serif typeface. It had the most sizes of any font (7!) and offered great readability. It lacks contrast until the 18px size, due to the limited number of pixels under 18px sizes.

Geneva

Inspired by Helvetica, Geneva was the typeface used in Finder. It's name aludes to Helvetica's Swiss origins, but is more condensed than Helvetica. It was designed for small point sizes

San Francisco

San Francisco is one of the weird typefaces of the original Macintosh. It was desgined to look as though each letter was cut out from a newspaper, creating a ransom note effect.

London

Completing the saga of weird fonts, London is a blackletter typeface. It has only one size.

Toronto

A geometric slab serif, it has no parallel in typography. However, because of it's apparent uselessness, it was removed after System 6.

Monaco

The only fixed-width typeface, it was used for programming, and has a modern outline version that is still in use.

Athens

A condensed slab-serif.

Venice

Inspired by calligraphy, Venice is the prettier sibling to Los Angeles. This is also the only font not designed by Susan Kare: it was designed by Bill Atkinson.

Los Angeles

The original Comic Sans, Los Angeles imitates handwriting, with a distincitve slope and irregular shapes, it is friendlier than other fonts on the Macintosh.
